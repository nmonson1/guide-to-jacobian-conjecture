# Sources and further reading

This page favors primary mathematical sources and executable repositories. A
link’s presence does not imply endorsement of every claim it contains.

## Start here

1. **[*A Counterexample to the Jacobian Conjecture*](https://www.ulam.ai/research/jacobian.pdf)**  
   The formula, exact verification, projective-root geometry, image,
   nonproperness set, and further families.

2. **[Secret Blogging Seminar: “The new counterexample to the Jacobian conjecture”](https://sbseminar.wordpress.com/2026/07/20/the-new-counterexample-to-the-jacobian-conjecture/)**  
   The best current collective English-language mathematical discussion.

3. **[Formal Conjectures PR #4474](https://github.com/google-deepmind/formal-conjectures/pull/4474)**  
   The shared Lean statement and review thread, with links to independent
   formalizations.

## Exact computation and exposition

- [jacobianfun](https://github.com/algal/jacobianfun): interactive exposition,
  exact scripts, and constructions of multiple generic degrees.
- [MMVFIRM verification package](https://github.com/MMVFIRM/alpoge-fable-jacobian-counterexample):
  frozen independent SymPy checks and reproducibility documentation.
- [Aaron Lou’s derivation](https://aaronlou.com/jacobian_counterexample_derivation.pdf):
  a reproducible route to the formula.
- [MathOverflow discussion of the cubic model and monodromy](https://mathoverflow.net/questions/513387/)
  and [constructions of other generic degrees](https://mathoverflow.net/questions/513390/).

## Classical background

- O.-H. Keller, “Ganze Cremona-Transformationen” (1939),
  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF01695502).
- H. Bass, E. H. Connell, and D. Wright, “The Jacobian conjecture:
  reduction of degree and formal expansion of the inverse” (1982),
  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1090/S0273-0979-1982-15032-7).
- L. M. Drużkowski, “An effective approach to Keller's Jacobian conjecture”
  (1983), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF01459126).
- A. Vistoli, “The Jacobian conjecture in dimension 3 and degree 3” (1999),
  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0022-4049(98)00040-1).
- A. van den Essen, *Polynomial Automorphisms and the Jacobian Conjecture*
  (2000), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-0348-8440-2).

## Source policy

When sources disagree, this guide should state the disagreement rather than
silently select the most convenient formulation. New references should say
exactly which claim or historical fact they support. See
[how to contribute](contribute.md).
